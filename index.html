Overview

The Nanchen Framework provides a structured methodology for building systematic understanding across philosophy, education, governance, and social reality. It emphasizes practical applicability, traceability, and auditable processes.

It integrates belief, value, philosophy, education, and institutional reality into a coherent model for long-term societal development.

Explore Models

Core Models

Belief → Value → Philosophy → Education → Institution/Reality

This model shows the cascading effect from foundational beliefs to practical societal structures. Each level informs and constrains the next, ensuring logical coherence and real-world applicability.

Sub-Truths and Rational Cognition Methodology

A method to verify completeness and truth of propositions, classifying statements, and providing stepwise rational operations applicable in philosophy, science, engineering, and social studies.

Power & Rights Standardization

Defines autonomous versus dependent types of power, grounds legitimacy in societal consensus, and enforces responsibilities and legal safeguards.

Rational Thinking Standards

Encourages awareness of cognitive limits, uses verifiable logic, conducts counter-checks, and systematically models complex systems for decision-making and prediction.
